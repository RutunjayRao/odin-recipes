Rice kheer

Description:

Rice Kheer (Chawal Ki Kheer) is a traditional Indian rice pudding that is quite easy to put together and makes for a great dessert.

So basically milk is the main ingredient of this dessert with other ingredients like rice, vermicelli, sago, lentils, etc added to it.Kheer is known by different names in different regions of India. In South India, it is called Payasam, or Payasa, while in Bengal it’s called Payesh.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp uncooked white rice
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 litle whole milk
  • raisins
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 teaspoons silvered almonds and cashew nuts

Steps to make:

  1. Rinse rice with water 2-3 times and soak it in ½ cup water for 30 minutes. Heat milk in a heavy bottom pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Keep stirring frequently to avoid scorching. When it comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low.
  3. Drain the water from the rice and add it to the pan along with saffron, raisins, and dry coconut.
  4. Cook uncovered on low heat until the rice is cooked and the kheer is thickened (45-50 minutes). While cooking, keep mashing with the back of the ladle.Keep stirring very frequently while cooking.
  5. Keep scraping the sides of the pan and add the solidified milk back to the pan.
  6. Keep in consideration that the kheer will thicken some more once cooled.
  7. Once the kheer has thickened, stir in the sugar and cardamom powder and cook for a minute. Check for sugar and add more if required and cook for another minute for it to dissolve.
  8. Transfer the kheer to small serving bowls. Garnish with almond and cashew nut slivers and dried rose petals and serve hot or chilled.
